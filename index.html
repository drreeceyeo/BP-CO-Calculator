Quick slider guide

What each slider changes
  • Heart Rate: beats per minute; very high HR reduces filling time → lowers SV.
  • Contractility: squeeze strength; higher = more SV per beat.
  • Blood Volume: preload; more stretch → more SV (until overloaded).
  • Venous Tone: gentle vein squeeze boosts blood return → more preload.
  • Arterial Radius: smaller radius → resistance ↑↑ (4th power) → MAP ↑.
  • Viscosity (η): thicker blood → resistance ↑ → MAP ↑.
  • Arterial Compliance (C): stiffer arteries (low C) → PP ↑ and SBP ↑.
  • Ventricular Compliance: a stiff ventricle fills poorly → SV ↓.
  • Regurgitation: % of stroke volume lost backward → forward CO ↓.

Common cardiovascular patterns (variable tendencies)

Hypertension (essential)
  • ↑ Arterial radius narrowing (↑TPR), often ↑ arterial stiffness (↓C)
  • MAP ↑; PP may widen with age/stiffness
Atherosclerosis / Aging arteries
  • ↓ Arterial compliance (stiffer); radius may be modestly reduced
  • PP ↑ (wide), SBP ↑; MAP ±
HFrEF (systolic heart failure)
  • ↓ Contractility, ↑ Blood volume (fluid retention), ↑ Venous tone (sympathetic)
  • SV ↓, CO ↓; MAP may fall unless TPR compensates
HFpEF (diastolic heart failure)
  • ↓ Ventricular compliance; often ↑ arterial stiffness
  • SV ↓ (filling impaired); PP often ↑
Atrial fibrillation (rapid)
  • Irregular, often fast HR → filling time ↓; loss of atrial kick
  • SV ↓; CO may drop despite higher HR
Aortic stenosis
  • Afterload ↑ (outflow obstruction); effective SV ↓
  • Often narrow PP; MAP may fall with exertion
Aortic regurgitation
  • Regurgitation ↑ → forward SV ↓; stroke volume large but wasted
  • Wide PP (high SBP, low DBP)
Anemia
  • ↓ Viscosity (η) → TPR ↓; compensatory ↑HR and ↑SV (hyperdynamic)
  • MAP often normal/low; PP may widen
Dehydration
  • ↓ Blood volume; ↑ Viscosity
  • SV ↓, CO ↓ → MAP ↓ (may see tachycardia)
Sepsis (early)
  • Marked vasodilation (↑ radius), low TPR; ↑ HR and ↑ contractility (catecholamines)
  • MAP may fall despite high CO
CKD/OSA-related hypertension
  • ↑ Volume (fluid retention), ↑ Venous tone/sympathetic drive, ↑ stiffness
  • MAP ↑; PP may widen
Thyroid disorders
  • Hyperthyroid: ↑ HR, ↑ contractility → CO ↑; TPR ↓
  • Hypothyroid: ↓ HR, ↓ contractility → CO ↓; TPR ↑
